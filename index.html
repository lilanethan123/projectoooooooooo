SECTION 7: HOW AMERICA IS BETTER NOW

How America Is Better Now

America is better today because technology has greatly improved daily life and transportation. Cars are now safer, faster, and cleaner compared to the dangerous and unreliable vehicles of the early 1900s. Just like indoor plumbing improved toilets and electricity improved the light bulb, modern car technology has made travel easier and more efficient. People now have more job opportunities in engineering, design, and technology industries related to automobiles. Strict societal expectations from the turn of the century, especially for women and workers, have also become more flexible. Economically, cars help people travel to better jobs, which strengthens the economy. Safety laws and environmental protections now help protect citizens and the planet. Overall, America's improvements in technology, opportunity, and safety show major progress since the early automobile era.
